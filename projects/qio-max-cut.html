MAX-CUT

A canonical QUBO problem: partition a graph into two sets to maximize the number (or weight) of cut edges.

Overview

MAX-CUT is a classic NP-hard graph optimization problem: split the vertex set into two groups so that the sum of weights of edges crossing the split is maximized.

It maps very naturally to an Ising/QUBO objective, which is why it’s one of the most common “hello world” benchmarks for quantum optimization (QAOA, annealing, etc.).

QUBO / Ising formulation

With binary variables indicating which side of the cut each node is on, the objective can be written as a quadratic function over binary/spin variables.

  • Input: graph $G=(V,E)$ (optionally weighted)
  • Output: partition of $V$ into two sets
  • Objective: maximize cut weight

QAOA approach

  • Problem Hamiltonian from the MAX-CUT objective
  • Mixer Hamiltonian to explore bitstrings
  • Classical optimizer to tune parameters $(\gamma,\beta)$

Classical baselines

  • Brute force for very small graphs
  • Local search / heuristics
  • Goemans–Williamson (for weighted graphs) as a strong approximation baseline (when included)
